ClimateAction.tech

A global community of tech professionals using our skills, expertise and platforms to help bring about solutions to the climate crisis.

About

The climate crisis requires a serious and sustained response from across civil society. The ClimateAction.tech mission is to empower technology professionals to play our part, to meet, discuss, learn and take climate action.

Our vision is that everyone is working on the climate crisis at all levels, and together we are driving industry and society toward a sustainable future.

Who we are

ClimateAction.tech was formed when a fairly random set of tech sector workers found each other and bonded on a shared vision to volunteer their own time and support and engage the entire tech sector in addressing the climate crisis.

Since then membership has ballooned and we have hundreds of highly active members, and counting.

Membership is open to anyone who identifies as a tech worker. People of any geographic region, sector, skillset, day job, seniority, knowledge or experience level are welcome, and we operate an intentionally safe and inclusive environment at all times.

People's Climate March Map

Members of ClimateAction.tech worked with the organizers of the People's Climate March to develop a map showing sister marchers across the country as well as transportation options.

In April over 200,000 people marched in Washington, DC. Some of them got there using our map.

Defund DAPL App

The Defund DAPL app helps you quickly learn if your bank is funding the Dakota Access Pipeline, and allows you to hold those banks accountable.

Emergency Room

Emergency Room is an immersive media installation comparing the way we respond to personal medical conditions with the way have responded to climate change. Twelve screens buzz and flicker with images of activism and environmental chaos, accompanied by a beeping heart-rate monitor.

Emergency Room features images of activists attempting to build momentum for change, alongside images of observed effects such as rising sea levels and increasing superstorms. Alongside these are images of individuals already dealing with the effects of climate change.

Learn more about the Emergency Room installation.

Employee Guide to Sustainability

We built a field guide for employees at tech companies about sustainability

By interviewing employees involved about sustainability across a cross-section of technology companies we were able to distill out best practices and the essentials one needs to know to be an effective internal sustainability advocate.

Sustainability Accelerator

We are running an accelerator program to help employees improve their company's carbon footprint. In the program employees collaborate with like-minded climate advocates and leading figures in other tech companies.

Participation is free and we are welcoming new advocates. To get involved, contact us now.
